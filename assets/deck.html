🌾 Crop Advisor Lite

AI crop advisory for Pakistan’s 8 million smallholder farmers — in English and Urdu.
Llama 4 Maverick · Fireworks AI (AMD-hosted inference) · Streamlit · Docker

The problem

  • 8M+ smallholder farms in Pakistan (1–12 acres) drive the country’s food supply.
  • Most farmers have no affordable access to agronomic advice: what to sow after harvest, when the sowing window closes, what an acre costs in inputs.
  • Government extension services are stretched thin; advice travels by word of mouth — often late, often wrong.
  • Bad crop decisions compound: one wrong season can wipe out a smallholder’s yearly margin.

The solution — a chat advisor in the farmer’s own language

I have 5 acres in Multan, just harvested wheat. Water is short. What should I sow next?
Crop Advisor Lite: With short water after wheat in Multan, consider mungbean (60–90 day crop, low water, fixes nitrogen) or sesame before the cotton window. Avoid rice this kharif — it needs far more water than a tubewell can affordably supply. Rough input cost for mungbean: PKR 25,000–40,000/acre…
  • Decision-ready answers: named crops, months, rupee ranges — not vague generalities.
  • English in, English out. Urdu in, Urdu out.
  • Honest about limits: refuses to invent market prices; routes high-stakes calls to extension offices.

What’s next

  • Grounding: retrieval over provincial extension guides and crop calendars, so every answer cites a real source.
  • Urdu voice: speech in/out for farmers who can’t read or type comfortably.
  • WhatsApp delivery: meet farmers on the platform they already use daily.
  • Cost path: open-weight models on AMD-hosted inference keep per-farmer cost near zero — the unit economics of free advice.
