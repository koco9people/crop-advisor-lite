# Wheat Cultivation in Pakistan 2025: Complete Yield, Cost, and Profit Guide

By[Times of Agriculture](https://www.timesofagriculture.pk)



## Introduction to Wheat Cultivation in Pakistan

Wheat cultivation in Pakistan 2025 shows how important this crop really is. Wheat isn’t just another crop in ***[Pakistan](http://www.google.com)***. It’s the backbone of the entire food system. Every year, millions of acres are planted, yet farmers still complain about low yields, rising input prices, and unpredictable weather. Here’s the thing: most of those issues come from outdated practices and poor planning, not fate.

If you get a firm grip on soil needs, choose the right seed, apply fertilizers at the right time, manage water properly, and stay ahead of diseases, you’ll already be farming better than most people around you. What follows is a straightforward, field-tested breakdown of what actually works, not the usual theory that never leaves the classroom.

## Climate and Soil Requirements

Wheat likes one thing above all: stability. It performs best in cool winters with moderate moisture and dry, warm conditions close to harvest.

#### Ideal conditions in Pakistan:

* Temperature at sowing: 20–25°C
* Temperature during tillering: 12–18°C
* Temperature during grain filling: 25–32°C
* Rainfall: 250–350 mm during the growing period

Soil type matters more than most farmers accept. Wheat hates compaction and waterlogging. What this really means is that you shouldn’t sow wheat in fields that were mismanaged during the rice season unless you first fix the soil.

#### Best soil types:

* Loam
* Sandy loam
* Clay loam with good drainage
* Soil pH: 6.5–7.5

If your field is alkaline, add organic matter if it’s compacted; deep plowing before sowing is non-negotiable.

## Land Preparation and Seedbed Management

Most farmers rush this part, then complain about weak germination. Don’t do that.

**A proper seedbed needs three things:**

1. Fine tilt
2. No clods
3. Uniform moisture

**Recommended steps:**

* First plowing with a deep plough or chisel plough after maize/rice.
* Two or three rotavator passes.
* Leveling with a laser land leveler for even sowing depth and irrigation savings.
* Pre-sowing irrigation (rauni) is required if the soil moisture is low.

You want a firm seedbed at the bottom and fine soil on top. That’s how you get uniform emergence.

## Certified Seed Rate and Sowing Methods

Seed rate depends on sowing time and method.

#### Ideal sowing time:

* 1–15 November in Punjab
* 1–30 November in Sindh
* 15 October–15 November in KP

Late sowing cuts yield like a knife. Every week after mid-November, you pay 10–12 kg per acre in grain filling.

#### Recommended seed rate:

* Drill sowing: 50–55 kg per acre
* Broadcasting: 60–65 kg per acre
* Late sowing: 60–70 kg per acre

#### Best sowing methods:

* Seed drill
* Zero-till drill after rice
* Happy seeder with rice straw
* Rabi drill + laser leveling

Avoid broadcasting if you actually care about yield. It wastes seed and causes uneven plant spacing.

## Fertilizer Schedule and Nutrient Management

***[Wheat](http://www.timesofagriculture.pk)*** can’t reach its full potential without a proper balance of nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium. The problem is that many farmers rely heavily on urea and ignore other nutrients, which limits yields from the start.

#### Recommended nutrient dose per acre:

* Nitrogen: 100–120 kg N (2.5 bags urea approx.)
* Phosphorus: 30–40 kg P (1.5–2 bags DAP or three bags SSP)
* Potash: 20–30 kg K (1 bag SOP or MOP in potash-deficient soils)

#### Split application schedule:

* At sowing: Full DAP + 1/3 urea
* First irrigation: 1/3 urea
* Second irrigation: Remaining urea

If you skip potash, expect weak stalks and shriveled grains. It’s that simple.

#### Micronutrients that matter:

* Zinc: 10 kg/acre
* Boron: 1–2 kg/acre

A deficiency of either one sharply reduces yield. Proper nutrient management is key for strong results in Wheat Cultivation in Pakistan 2025.

## Irrigation Requirements and Water Scheduling

Wheat doesn’t need heavy irrigation, but it does need timely irrigation.

#### Critical irrigation stages:

1. Crown root initiation (CRi) – 20–25 days after sowing
2. Tillering – 45–55 days
3. Booting – 70–80 days
4. Grain filling – 100–110 days

Miss any of these and you will see a drop in tillers, reduced plant height, and lower grain weight.  
Flood irrigation works, but laser leveling saves 25–30 percent of water. Drip is an overkill for wheat unless you’re in a highly water-scarce area.

#### Weed Management Strategies

Wheat loses 30–40 percent of its yield from weeds if you let them grow unchecked.

#### Major weeds:

* Bathu
* Jangli jowar
* Krund
* Lehli
* Dumbi sitti

The cleanest approach is simple:

#### Use weedicides at the right time:

* Broadleaf weeds: Bromoxynil + MCPA
* Narrow leaf weeds: Clodinafop
* Mixed weed infestation: Sulfosulfuron

Apply weedicides 30–40 days after sowing. And don’t use dirty water during spray; it kills herbicide effectiveness.

## Major Wheat Diseases and Their Control

Wheat diseases hit silently, and by the time farmers notice them, damage is already done.

#### Common diseases:

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **Diseases** | **Control** |
| Rust (yellow, brown, black) | Use resistant varieties + fungicide (propiconazole or tebuconazole) |
| Smut | Seed treatment with carboxin or mancozeb |
| Karnal bunt | Crop rotation + seed treatment |
| Leaf blight | Avoid late sowing + foliar fungicides |

Small farmers often skip seed treatment, which is a mistake. It prevents half the early-season problems for almost no extra cost.

## Insect Pests and Practical Control Measures:

Pakistan’s wheat crop doesn’t face as many pests as cotton does, but a few can still ruin yields.

#### Common pests:

* Armyworm
* Aphids
* Wheat stem borer

#### Control tips:

* Use yellow sticky traps for aphids.
* Spray imidacloprid at early infestation.
* Avoid excessive nitrogen; it attracts aphids like a magnet.
* Deep plowing reduces stem borer populations.

## Harvesting, Threshing, and Post-Harvest Practices

Don’t rush harvesting. If you harvest too early, moisture will ruin storage.

#### Harvest wheat when:

* Grain moisture: 12–14 percent
* Grains are hard and scratch-free
* Straw is golden and dry

Combine harvesting is fine, but adjust settings to reduce grain breakage. Clean and dry grains before storage.

## Wheat Yield Per Acre in Pakistan:

Let’s cut the fantasies. The average yield in Pakistan is nowhere near what it could be. Here are the real numbers:

* **Average farmer yield:** 28–32 maunds per acre
* **Good management yields:** 35–45 maunds per acre
* **High-performance farms:** 50–60 maunds per acre
* **Top researcher-managed plots:** 70+ maunds

If someone tells you they got 70 maunds without proper nutrient and irrigation management, they’re exaggerating. Most yield losses in Wheat Cultivation in Pakistan 2025 are due to poor management practices, not to the crop itself.

## Common Farmer Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

These are the mistakes that drag yields down every single season:

1. Late sowing
2. Using saved seed
3. Overuse of urea and ignoring potash
4. Poor land leveling
5. Delayed weedicides
6. Skipping seed treatment
7. Harvesting too early

Fix these, and your yield goes up automatically without spending extra money.
